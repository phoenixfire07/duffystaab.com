Kevin Thomas Duffy

U.S.D.J. Ret.
Of Counsel

(203) 531-3300

JudgeDuffy@duffystaab.com

Kevin Thomas Duffy joined Duffy & Staab LLC after retiring on September 30, 2016, following 44 years of service as a U.S. District Judge in the Southern District of New York.

Judge Duffy began his career in the U. S. Attorneys’ Office for the S.D.N.Y. From 1961 to 1969 he was in private practice. He was then called back to government service as Regional Administrator of the SEC’s New York Region where some of his suggestions led to the formation of the Securities Investor Protection Corporation as well as to the implementation of many managerial changes which were later adopted by the rest of the SEC.

He was nominated to the federal bench by President Richard Nixon in 1972. Upon his confirmation by the Senate he became the youngest member of the federal judiciary.

Judge Duffy’s judicial career included presiding over a many famous cases, among which were U.S. v. Tramunti, U.S. v. Castellano, the trial of those responsible for the first terrorist bombing of the World Trade Center and other terrorist cases flowing from that matter, and the prosecution of the Black Liberation Army (a/k/a the Brinks trial).

Among Judge Duffy’s most notable civil cases were the Iranian Assets Cases, involving $98 billion worth of gold held by the New York Federal Reserve Bank.

Since joining Duffy & Staab LLC, Judge Duffy is concentrating on arbitrations and mediations and on assisting in firm litigation matters. He is also available for counseling on regulatory matters, including those involving the SEC and CFTC.
